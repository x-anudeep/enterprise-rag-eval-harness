HIPAA breach notification procedures require covered entities to assess impermissible uses or
disclosures of protected health information. The assessment should consider the nature of the
information, the unauthorized person who used or received it, whether the information was actually
acquired or viewed, and the extent to which risk was mitigated.

When a breach is confirmed, affected individuals must be notified without unreasonable delay and
no later than sixty calendar days after discovery. Business associates must notify the covered
entity so the covered entity can meet individual, media, and regulator notification obligations.
