Covered entities must make reasonable efforts to use, disclose, and request only the
minimum necessary protected health information needed to accomplish the intended purpose.
Role-based access controls should limit workforce access to records based on job function.
Organizations must document policies for routine disclosures and review non-routine requests
individually before releasing protected health information.

The minimum necessary standard does not apply to disclosures for treatment, disclosures made
to the individual, uses or disclosures made pursuant to a valid authorization, or disclosures
required by law. Privacy officers should train workforce members on these exceptions and keep
training records available for audit.
